The Impact of Seasonal Variations on Water Quality in Delhi

Seasonal variations in Delhi significantly affect water quality, with monsoon rains exacerbating the pollution levels in local water bodies. During the monsoon season, increased runoff from urban and rural areas washes pollutants into rivers and lakes, leading to elevated levels of contaminants and a higher risk of waterborne diseases

Delhi’s Riverfronts: A Case Study of Pollution in the Yamuna River

The Yamuna River, a vital water source for Delhi, is plagued by severe pollution, primarily due to untreated sewage and industrial effluents. The river’s water quality has deteriorated to alarming levels, with high concentrations of pathogens and pollutants that render it unsafe for drinking and recreational use

Community Efforts and Grassroots Movements Against Water Pollution in Delhi

In response to Delhi's water pollution crisis, numerous grassroots movements and community organizations have emerged, striving to make a difference at the local level. Initiatives such as river clean-up drives, public awareness campaigns have shown promising results in certain areas.
